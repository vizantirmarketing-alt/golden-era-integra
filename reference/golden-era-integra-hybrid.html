Origin Story
起源

In 1995, Honda built
the perfect
front-wheel-drive
chassis.

The third-generation Integra — internally known as DC2 — represented a high-water mark in Honda's engineering culture. It was light. It was honest. The B18C1 revved to 8,100 RPM with the kind of mechanical clarity that has all but disappeared from modern cars.

This particular example sat in a single-car garage in Pasadena for twenty-six years. Two owners. Eighty-seven thousand miles. Original Milano Red paint, original interior, untouched powertrain. The kind of starting point most enthusiasts only read about.
